As in the Triple Dictator Game, you are randomly assigned the role of player A or player B. You will play the role of Player A or B for 10 rounds and then switch to being the other Player for another 10 rounds. One random round will be chosen for payments. It is important to note that this stage of the study determines a big part of your payment and that all money amounts involved in this stage are real.

Player A is given {{10|c}} which they decide how to allocate between themselves and Player B. Player B starts off with {{0|c}} and receives triple the amount Player A decides to share with player B. Note that in this game, Player B can choose to return part or all of the money received from Player A. The following are some example scenarios.

Ex1. Player A has {{10|c}} and player B has {{0|c}}. Player A decides to give player B {{5|c}}. Player B receives 3 * {{5|c}} = {{15|c}}. Player B can then chooses to return {{5|c}} of the {{15|c}}. At the end of the round, player A gets {{10|c}} as payoff and Player B also gets {{10|c}} as payoff.

Ex.2 Player A has {{10|c}} and player B has {{0|c}}. Player A doesn't want to give player B any of the {{10|c}}. Round ends and Player A keeps {{10|c}} as payoff while Player B receives {{0|c}} as payoff.

Ex3. Player A has {{10|c}} and player B has {{0|c}}. Player A gives all of the {{10|c}} to Player B. Player B receives 3 * {{10|c}} = {{30|c}}. Player B then decides to return none of the {{30|c}} to Player A. Round ends, Player A has {{0|c}} as payoff while Player B gets {{30|c}} as payoff.

Ex.4 Player A has {{10|c}} and player B has {{0|c}}. Player A gives all of the {{10|c}} to Player B. Player B receives 3 * {{10|c}} = {{30|c}}. Player B then decides to return half of the {{30|c}} to Player A. Round ends, Player A has {{15|c}} as payoff and Player B also gets {{15|c}} as payoff.

Remember the money involved in this game is real and constitute is a big portion of your payment. You will be asked to answer the following question. As player A, what percentage of the money given to player B do you expect they will return?

Ex.1 If you give Player B {{10|c}} they receive {{30|c}} if they give you back {{30|c}} then that is 300% of what you sent them.

Ex.2 If you give Player B {{5|c}} they receive 3 * 5 = {{15|c}} and they give you back {{5|c}} then that is 100% return of what you sent them

Ex.3 If you give Player B {{7|c}} they receive 3* {{7|c}} = {{21|c}} and they give you none of it back then that is a 0% return of what you sent them.

Ex.4 If you give Player B {{3|c}} they receive 3 * {{3|c}} = {{9|c}} and they give you {{2|c}} of it back then that is roughly 66% of what you sent them.

Lastly, remember that the money involved is real and heavily impacts the payment for participants at the end. The allocation of the money involved is completely up to you.
